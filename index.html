List of equipment of the Philippine Air Force

The following is a list of current and former aircraft of the Philippine Air Force.

1. FA-50PH

FA-50PH of the 7th Tactical Fighter Squadron

The KAI T-50 Golden Eagle is a family of South Korean supersonic advanced trainers and multirole light fighters, developed by Korea Aerospace Industries with Lockheed Martin. The T-50 is South Korea's first indigenous supersonic aircraft and one of the world's few supersonic trainers.[6] Development began in the late 1990s, and its maiden flight occurred in 2002. The aircraft entered active service with the Republic of Korea Air Force in 2005.

2. F-8H Crusader

A Vought F-8H Crusader of the Philippine Air Force in flight.

The Philippine Air Force operated some 25 F-8Hs from 1978. However, the aircraft quickly became too expensive and difficult to maintain. Due to the lack of any immediate "enemy", the Crusaders were permanently grounded in 1988 stored at Basa Air Base.

3. F-5A

F-5A Philippine AF at Clark AB 1982.

A Philippine Air Force Northrop F-5A Freedom Fighter aircraft on the flight line during the air combat training exercise "Cope Thunder '83-1" at Clark Air Base on 1 November 1982.

4. F-86 Sabre

F 86 SABRE (1957-1979) PHILIPPINE AIR FORCE

The Philippine Air Force first received the Sabres in the form of F-86Fs in 1957, replacing the North American P-51 Mustang as the Philippine Air Force's primary interceptor. F-86s first operated from Basa Air Base, known infamously as the Nest of Vipers where the 5th Fighter Wing of the PAF was based. Later on, in 1960, the PAF acquired the F-86D as the first all weather interceptor of the PAF. The most notable use of the F-86 Sabres was in the Blue Diamonds aerobatic display team, which operated eight Sabres until the arrival of the newer, supersonic Northrop F-5. The F-86s were subsequently phased out of service in the 1970s.

5. P-51 Mustang

PAF P-51 Mustang

The main aircraft type in the earlier era of the PAF was the P-51 Mustang, flown from 1947 to 1959. Ground attack missions were flown against various insurgent groups, with aircraft hit by ground fire but none shot down. In the 1950s the Mustang was used by the Blue Diamonds aerobatic display team.

6. OV-10 Bronco

Balikatan 2019 - Philippine Air Force's OV-10 Light Armed Reconnaissance Aircraft

The OV-10 Bronco is a fixed-wing attack aircraft that the PAF uses for surface strike missions in support of focused military operations. It has been particularly effective in counter-insurgency operations, as they can loiter for a long time and provide close air support to ground troops when needed.

7. Embraer A-29 Super Tucano

Philippine Air Force A-29B Super Tucano Attack Aircraft

The Embraer A-29 Super Tucano (English: Super Toucan), also named EMB 314 or ALX, is a Brazilian turboprop light attack aircraft designed and built by Embraer as a development of the Embraer EMB 312 Tucano. It is powered by a more powerful 1,600 shp (1,200 kW) Pratt & Whitney Canada PT6A-68C engine (compared to the EMB-312’s 750 shp (560 kW) powerplant) The A-29 Super Tucano carries a wide variety of weapons, including precision-guided munitions, and was designed to be a low-cost system operated in low-threat environments.In addition to its manufacture in Brazil, Embraer has set up a production line in the United States in conjunction with Sierra Nevada Corporation for the manufacture of A-29s to export customers.

8. C-130H

Philippine Air Force C-130H delivered

This C-130H, bearing tail number 5157, marks the second of two C-130 aircraft granted by the US Government through the Excess Defense Articles (EDA) Program. The first unit was delivered on January 29, 2021, and formally turned over to the PAF in February 2021.

9. F/A-18CD Hornet

The Phil. Air Force was considering the F/A-18 C/D Hornet but these plans were scrapped because of the 1997 Asian Financial Crisis | According to MaxDefense, 12 to 24 units were planned.

22 F/A-18C/D Super Hornets were ordered and 6 were currently delivered to the Philippine Air Force to provide air defense and to replace the agng F-8S Crusaders that had been in service since 1977. The 7th Tactical Fighter 'Bulldogs' Squadron operating out of Basa AFB were equipped with the type, operating in a mixture of the air defense and secondary strike mission roles. Fitted with the Raytheon AN/APG-79 Active electronically scanned array (ESA) radar, the F/A-18 Super Hornet could be equipped for in-flight refueling which it can be refueling from their newer Embraer KC-390's if needed. Standard armament in the air-to-air/interceptor role is the 20 mm M61A2 Vulcan and 4 AIM-9 Sidewinders in additionto 4 AIM-7 Sparrow missiles.

The MRF is part of Horizon 2 of the AFP Modernization Program from 2018 to 2022,

Which aims to acquire more equipment for external defense. Any aircraft that will be selected must be able to integrate with existing radar systems that have a range of about 250 nautical miles. According to local media, the leading contenders for the MRF programme are Lockheed Martin’s F-16V and the Saab’s Gripen C/D.

2. JAS39 Gripen

Sweden's developed JAS39 "Gripen" Multi Role Combat Aircraft

The Saab JAS 39 Gripen is a light single-engine supersonic multirole fighter aircraft manufactured by the Swedish aerospace and defence company Saab AB. The Gripen has a delta wing and canard configuration with relaxed stability design and fly-by-wire flight controls. Later aircraft are fully NATO interoperable.
